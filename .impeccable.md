## Design Context

### Users
Blockkit is primarily used by content managers creating, organizing, and updating website content inside the CMS. Secondary users are developers defining and evolving content types for editors to use, plus marketing stakeholders who need to manage SEO and related website metadata. The core job to be done is creating and editing content quickly without getting lost in a cluttered admin interface.

### Brand Personality
Fast, efficient, understandable.

The interface should make users feel capable and in control rather than impressed for its own sake. It should reduce friction, expose structure clearly, and avoid the usual CMS pattern of dense, noisy navigation and overloaded forms.

### Aesthetic Direction
Utilitarian, subtle, and refined rather than flashy. The product should deliberately move away from conventional cluttered CMS UI patterns and favor clear structure, disciplined hierarchy, and calm visual restraint. Support both light and dark mode. Colors should stay subtle and polished, avoiding loud or novelty-driven accents.

### Design Principles
1. Efficiency over ornament: prioritize fast scanning, direct actions, and low-friction editing flows.
2. Clarity through structure: make hierarchy, navigation, and content relationships obvious at a glance.
3. Restraint with intent: keep the palette, motion, and decoration subtle, but ensure the interface still feels carefully designed rather than generic.
4. Editors first, developers supported: optimize day-to-day editing while preserving clear affordances for schema-aware and SEO-oriented work.
5. Accessible by default: respect accessibility expectations, including reduced motion, while maintaining a coherent and usable interface in every mode.
